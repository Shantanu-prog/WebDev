Linus Torvalds

The creator of Linux

Biography

The first Linux prototypes were publicly released on the Internet in late 1991 from an FTP server at his university.[8][24] Version 1.0 was released on 14 March 1994.[25] Torvalds first encountered the GNU Project in the autumn of 1991 when another Swedish-speaking computer science student, Lars Wirzenius, took him to the University of Technology to listen to free software guru Richard Stallman's speech. Because of the talk and pressure from other contributors, Torvalds would ultimately switch his original license (which forbade commercial use) to Stallman's GNU General Public License version 2 (GPLv2) for his Linux kernel.

Achievements

  • 2019 Great Immigrants Award: Honored by the Carnegie Corporation of New York.
  • 2018 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award: Recognized for outstanding contributions to consumer electronics technology.
  • 2014 IEEE Computer Pioneer Award: Presented by the IEEE Computer Society for pioneering work.
  • 2012 Internet Hall of Fame: Inducted as an Innovator
  • 2012 Millennium Technology Prize: Shared with Shinya Yamanaka, described as technology's equivalent of the Nobel Prize.
  • 2010 C&C Prize: Awarded by the NEC Corporation for contributions to IT and education.
  • 2008 Hall of Fellows: Inducted into the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California.
  • 2005 Vollum Award: Received from Reed College[9].
  • 2003 Takeda Award: Shared with Richard Stallman and Ken Sakamura for social/economic well-being[10].
  • 2000 Lovelace Medal: Awarded by the British Computer Society[11].
  • 1998 EFF Pioneer Award: Recognized by the Electronic Frontier Foundation[12].
